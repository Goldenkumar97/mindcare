Frequently Asked Questions

Q: I feel stressed all the time. Is this normal?
A: Yes. Stress is common in studies, work, and life. But if stress stays for many days and affects sleep or daily life, you should seek help.

Q: How do I know if I need professional help?
A: If you feel sad, anxious, or hopeless most days, or have trouble sleeping, eating, or focusing, it is a good idea to talk to a counselor or doctor.

Q: Can listening to music really help my mind?
A: Yes. Calm music can slow breathing, reduce anxiety, and help your brain relax. It works best with slow breathing.

Q: Is it okay to feel sad without any clear reason?
A: Yes. Sometimes emotions come without a clear cause. It does not mean you are weak. It means your mind needs care and rest.

Q: What should I do if I feel very anxious right now?
A: Try slow breathing (inhale 4s, hold 4s, exhale 6s), drink water, and move away from noise. If anxiety feels out of control, contact a helpline.

Q: Will my message on this website be seen by others?
A: No. Messages are private and only visible to the website owner. Your information is not shared publicly.

Q: Can this website replace a doctor or therapist?
A: No. This website is for awareness and support only. It cannot replace professional medical advice or treatment.

Q: What if I am helping a friend who feels suicidal?
A: Stay with them, listen calmly, and encourage them to call a helpline or visit a hospital. Do not leave them alone.

Q: Is feeling tired and unmotivated a mental health issue?
A: It can be related to stress, poor sleep, or emotional pressure. If it continues for weeks, professional advice is recommended.

Q: How long does it take to feel better?
A: It depends on the person and situation. Small steps like sleep, talking, breathing, and music help, but healing takes time.

Q: Can students use this website?
A: Yes. Students often feel pressure from studies and future worries. This website is safe for students to use.

Q: What should I do if I feel lonely?
A: Try talking to someone you trust, join a small activity, or message someone. Loneliness reduces when you connect with others.

Q: Is this website a doctor?
A: No. This website only provides awareness and support information.

Q: Are my messages private?
A: Yes, only visible to the site owner.

Q: What should I do in emergency?
A: Call emergency numbers immediately or visit the nearest hospital.
